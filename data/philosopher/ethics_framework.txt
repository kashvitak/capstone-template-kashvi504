Ethical Analysis Framework for Technology

1. CORE ETHICAL PRINCIPLES
   - Autonomy: Respect for individual choice and self-determination
   - Beneficence: Acting in the best interest of others
   - Non-maleficence: "First, do no harm"
   - Justice: Fair distribution of benefits and burdens
   - Privacy: Protection of personal information and dignity

2. TECHNOLOGY ETHICS QUESTIONS
   - Who benefits? Who might be harmed?
   - Does this increase or decrease human autonomy?
   - What are the unintended consequences?
   - Does this exacerbate existing inequalities?
   - Is informed consent possible and meaningful?

3. COMMON ETHICAL PITFALLS
   - Surveillance and privacy erosion
   - Algorithmic bias and discrimination
   - Addiction and manipulation (dark patterns)
   - Environmental degradation
   - Job displacement without support systems
   - Concentration of power and wealth

4. ETHICAL FRAMEWORKS
   - Utilitarianism: Greatest good for greatest number
   - Deontology: Duty-based ethics, universal rules
   - Virtue ethics: Character and moral excellence
   - Care ethics: Relationships and responsibilities
   - Rights-based: Fundamental human rights protection

5. RESPONSIBLE INNOVATION PRINCIPLES
   - Transparency in design and operation
   - Accountability for outcomes
   - Inclusivity in development process
   - Sustainability and long-term thinking
   - Reversibility when possible
