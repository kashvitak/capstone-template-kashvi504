Market Analysis and Economic Viability Framework

1. MARKET SIZING METHODOLOGY
   - TAM (Total Addressable Market): All potential customers
   - SAM (Serviceable Available Market): Realistic target segment
   - SOM (Serviceable Obtainable Market): Achievable in 3-5 years
   - Growth rate projections (CAGR)
   - Market maturity stage (emerging, growth, mature, declining)

2. COST STRUCTURE ANALYSIS
   - COGS (Cost of Goods Sold): materials, labor, overhead
   - Fixed costs: R&D, facilities, equipment
   - Variable costs: per-unit production, distribution
   - Customer acquisition cost (CAC)
   - Lifetime value (LTV) and LTV:CAC ratio

3. COMPETITIVE LANDSCAPE
   - Direct competitors (same solution, same market)
   - Indirect competitors (different solution, same problem)
   - Substitutes (alternative ways to solve the problem)
   - Barriers to entry (patents, capital, network effects)
   - Competitive advantages (cost, quality, brand, distribution)

4. ADOPTION BARRIERS
   - Regulatory hurdles (approvals, certifications)
   - Cultural resistance to change
   - High switching costs for customers
   - Lack of complementary infrastructure
   - Economic conditions (recession, inflation)

5. REVENUE MODELS
   - One-time purchase (hardware, software license)
   - Subscription (SaaS, recurring revenue)
   - Freemium (free basic, paid premium)
   - Marketplace/platform (transaction fees)
   - Advertising or data monetization

6. FINANCIAL METRICS
   - Break-even point (units or time)
   - Gross margin (revenue - COGS)
   - Operating margin (after all expenses)
   - Return on investment (ROI)
   - Payback period for customers
